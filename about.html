Cal Gymnastics Club is a volunteer run organization by UC Berkeley students, staff, alumni and the general community.

We offer practice of artistic gymnastics for both recreational and competitive gymnasts, even with no previous experience. We compete as part of the National Association of Intercollegiate Gymnastics Clubs (NAIGC).

Responsibilites

President

  • Schedule officer and club meetings
  • Make sure that officers do their duties on time and correctly
  • Make sure that anything that needs to get done, gets finished
  • Be in communication with officers, members and Sport Clubs Office
  • Find the required number of members/ officers to attend the Sport Clubs meetings. Insure that they will and have attended.
  • Inform safety officer of purchase orders
  • Call and preside over all Club meetings and executive meetings
  • Supervise and oversee all Club activitiesRegulate the use and control of all Club property and the facility
  • Access volunteer coaches application and communicate options with Council
  • Get volunteer coaches registered with the RSF
  • Act as, or find, an MC (Master of Ceremonies) for all home meets
  • Acting as contact for all teams, as well as prospective new members
  • Fulfill or delegate all obligations for vacant officer positions

Vice President

  • Assume the presidential responsibilities in the absence of the president
  • Communicate with President about which jobs are falling behind and re-delegate those duties to other officers
  • Secure judges and facilities for home meets
  • Communicate with Sport Clubs Office and Golden Bear Recreational Facility director (currently Abra Slater)
  • Maintain and edit Code of Conduct and Constitution (after approved by Council or necessary people)
  • Get coaches for Blue and Gold teams
  • Oversee payment approvals for Gold Team Coach with the Treasure
  • Reply to Club emails
  • Create Nationals Planning Committee with President
  • Initiate Officers training with Council
  • Approve Budget with President
  • Assist President with any duties that need attention.
  • Communicate with President about which jobs are falling behind and re-delegate those duties to other officers.

Treasurer

  • Verify membership purchases
  • Maintain the Club’s financial records
  • Submit purchase orders and travel reimbursement requests
  • Obtain and disperse Club funds for any Club activity/ purchase
  • Prepare the Club’s annual budget for the Sport Clubs Program
  • Maintain an up-to-date budget year round
  • Deposits to sports club office after fundraisers (PNOs, Flips for Tips, etc.)
  • Contact Sport Clubs office about change in membership dues
  • Maintain online Club store

Secretary

  • Maintain and organize club documentation (sign in sheets, rosters, competition attendees (with team captains), volunteer hours (with fundraising chair), finances (with treasurer)).
  • ​Maintain club calendar with all club related events (competitions, meetings, fundraisers)
  • ​Notify everyone of events and their roles/duties. Make sure they are executing everything on time to meet desired plans
  • ​​Organize agenda of officer and club meetings, make sure agenda is executed, write reports of proposed and decided changes
  • ​​Publish minutes online and update all internal and public documentation with proposed changes.
  • ​Double check club mailing list and online communication (missing attachments, wrong links, typos …)
  • ​​General help to the president, vice presidents and other officers in cases of emergency.

Team Captain

  • Serve as Gold Team Captains for all meets
  • Order and keep track of club uniforms
  • Recruit gymnasts for the club’s competitive team

Fundraising Chair

  • Create flyers to promote upcoming events two weeks prior to event. Remind Building Director of Golden Bear Recreational Center to distribute flyer and help advertise events
  • At least one fundraising chair must be present at PNO
  • Organize Flip for Tips
  • Brainstorming new ideas to increase club income
  • In charge of keeping track of all members volunteer hours in coordination with secretary
  • Helping motivate members to doing their share of hours (minimum 12 but try to make everyone have more so even distribution of work given so even money distribution)

Event Planner

  • In charge of decorations, set up, awards, and tear down of home meets
  • Organize end of year banquet
  • Plan additional club activities for team bonding
  • Coordinate with University activities for more team bonding
  • Share responsibilities with PR for tabling events
  • Coordinate with PR for publicizing upcoming meets to Berkeley community
  • Book/plan sleeping arrangements for away meets. Get info from Team Captains
  • Help away teams book rooms/amenities any year West Coast Regionals is held in Berkeley or for home meets with further away schools like UCLA.

Public Relations

  • Update social media sites
  • Emails regarding all club, distribute info to all club
  • Organize club recruitment: Caltopia/ Cal Day/Calapolooza
  • Contact outside clubs (excel programs) get more members from high schoolers going into college.
  • Brainstorm new ideas on how to get more people to know about the club/ get more members
  • Flyering on campus Sproul
  • Ad on tvs at RSF
  • Anything else to solicit beginner’s classes
  • Advertise to community for upcoming meets

Historian

  • Make updates to the webpage and social profiles with current information (class schedule, opening times, cancellations …)
  • Take pictures and videos at practices and meets
  • Keep website updated with those pictures/ videos

Safety Officer

  • Make sure people are CPR and first aid trained including
  • Make sure that all club events has at least one member trained (all meets, practices, PNOs, sleepovers, etc.)
  • Make sure the First Aid kit in the cabinet below vault runway is stocked and up to date including competition medical supplies, i.e. tape
  • In charge of picking up delivered medical items from RSF
  • In charge of bringing the safety bag on every trip, regardless if the other gym has a kit
  • Secure trainer for home meets
  • Keep a spreadsheet of officer cpr trained expiration dates so we know

West Region Coordinator

  • Attend all West Region phone conferences
  • Communicate with other West Coast Universities the meet schedule for the year
  • Fulfill any other duties that may come up
